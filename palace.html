Wollemi Pine Trees and Gardens

The gardens are home to the rare and ancient Wollemi pine trees, one of the world’s oldest and most significant plant species. Thought to be extinct until their rediscovery in 1994, Wollemi pines are living fossils dating back over 200 million years. Their survival is of great scientific and conservation importance, and they are protected by strict regulations. The landscaped gardens also feature a variety of native plants.

For the health and preservation of these unique trees, please do not enter the garden beds or allow pets and animals near the Wollemi pines. Respecting these guidelines helps ensure the continued survival of this remarkable species for future guests to enjoy and appreciate.
